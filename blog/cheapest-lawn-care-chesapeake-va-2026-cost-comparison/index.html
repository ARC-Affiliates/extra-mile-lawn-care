How Much Should I Pay for Bi-Weekly Lawn Care?

Bi-weekly lawn care is a service schedule where the crew mows, edges, and blows clippings every 14 days during the growing season.

Expect $90–$160 per month for bi-weekly mowing on a typical Chesapeake residential lot in 2026.

That works out to two visits monthly at roughly $45–$80 each. Bi-weekly is cheaper upfront than weekly, but warm-season grasses like Bermuda and zoysia — common across South Norfolk and Greenbrier — grow fast in June and July. Stretching to 14-day intervals can stress turf and force the crew to bag clippings, which sometimes adds a fee.

Weekly service costs more per month but produces healthier turf and lower per-cut prices, while bi-weekly saves money but can mean uneven cuts and extra hauling charges during peak growth.

Do You Need a License for Landscaping in Virginia?

A landscaping license in Virginia is a state credential required for certain regulated services like pesticide application and large contracting jobs.

Basic mowing requires no state license, but pesticide and herbicide application requires certification through the Virginia Department of Agriculture.

Legitimate Chesapeake lawn care providers should carry:

  • VDACS Pesticide Business License — required for any company applying weed control or fertilizer with regulated chemicals (vdacs.virginia.gov).
  • Virginia Class A, B, or C Contractor License — required for landscape construction jobs over $1,000 / $10,000 / $120,000 respectively, per Va. Code § 54.1-1100 (law.lis.virginia.gov).
  • General liability insurance — minimum $300,000–$1,000,000 per occurrence is standard.
  • Workers' compensation — required in Virginia for any business with two or more employees.

How to Compare Lawn Care Quotes the Right Way

Compare quotes by frequency, scope, and credentials — not just dollar amount.

Quote-comparison checklist

  1. Confirm visit frequency (weekly vs. bi-weekly vs. monthly).
  2. Verify cut height and whether clippings are bagged or mulched.
  3. Ask if edging and blowing are included or extra.
  4. Request proof of liability insurance and VDACS license if chemicals are involved.
  5. Check whether the price is per visit, per month, or per season.
  6. Ask about rain delays and missed-cut policies.
  7. Get the scope and price in writing before the first visit.
